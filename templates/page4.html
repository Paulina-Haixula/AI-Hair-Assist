Step 3 — Damage & Condition

These indicators help assess hair damage severity, breakage risk, and potential underlying scalp or hair health concerns.

Edges are the most fragile hairline areas and are sensitive to tension, styling, and breakage.
The Hamilton–Norwood Scale is the clinical standard for diagnosing and tracking male pattern baldness progression.
Class 1: No significant hair loss or recession.
Class 2: Slight recession at the temples (mature hairline).
Class 3: First clinically significant hair loss with deep recession in an M, U, or V shape; possible crown thinning.
Class 4: Severe recession and a sparse or bald crown, separated by a band of hair.
Class 5: Enlarged balding areas with a thinner separating band.
Class 6: Temple and crown balding areas merge.
Class 7: Most severe stage; only a thin horseshoe-shaped band remains around the sides and back.
Breakage reflects strand fragility due to dryness, chemical processing, or mechanical stress.
Overall condition reflects hydration balance, structural integrity, and recent damage exposure.
